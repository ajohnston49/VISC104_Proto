Getting Started with Emulation

Everything you need to play classic console games on your computer—without the confusion.

🛠️ How do I set up an emulator?

Download one from the Emulation Lair, unzip it, and open the executable. Most emulators work out-of-the-box, but make sure your system meets basic requirements (Windows 10+, DirectX installed).

📦 Where do I put ROM files?

Once you've downloaded a game from The Vault, place it in a folder of your choice. Then, from inside the emulator, select "Open ROM" and point to that file. Boom—retro magic activated.

🎮 What controller should I use?

USB controllers work best. We recommend Xbox-style for compatibility, or retro USB models if you want that authentic feel. Emulators usually have a "Configure Input" section to map buttons.

🧑‍🔧 Why won’t my ROM load?

Check the file extension (.nes, .gba, .sfc, etc.). Make sure the emulator supports that console. Also double-check that the ROM isn’t zipped or corrupted—re-download if needed.

📺 How do I make it look better?

Open video settings and enable filters like HQ2x, CRT shaders, or smooth scaling. You can also adjust window size or fullscreen mode in most emulators.

📝 Can I save my progress?

Yep! Use "Save States" to capture your exact moment in-game. Later, hit "Load State" and pick up right where you left off—no need for in-game passwords or saves.
